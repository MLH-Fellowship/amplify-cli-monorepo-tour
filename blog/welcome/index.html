Major League Hacking Fellows Recap of Summer 2020 Program

September 2, 2020 ¬∑ 6 min read

Matt Auerbach

Sr. Solutions Architect

Building Amplify as Open Source Software has given us many advantages ‚Äî for one, it‚Äôs enabled us to accelerate development and work alongside our developers. We are always on the lookout for initiatives to grow our open source community.

As COVID started to affect student internships Worldwide, the folks at Major League Hacking (MLH) started to crank away on a clever alternative ‚Äî pair eager and passionate student technologists with open source libraries who are interested in mentoring and investing in first time contributors. The MLH Fellowship was born. They narrowed the inaugural class to 144 from receiving over 20,000 applicants.

This summer we had the pleasure of mentoring and working with four MLH fellows. We split the students by assigning half to our JavaScript Repo and the other half to our CLI Repo.

The students worked diligently throughout the summer alongside three Amplify Software Development Engineers to make open source contributions that benefit Amplify customers and the wider community. In the spirit of open source, their work can be viewed on this GitHub Project. In total, the fellows closed out over 40+ GitHub issues!

During the past three months, I‚Äôve been actively contributing to the AWS Amplify CLI repo.

As a fellow, I contributed to AWS Amplify‚Äôs CLI. I spent most of my time helping other engineers build a new feature that allows users to write serverless functions in the Swift language and deploy them to AWS Lambda.
